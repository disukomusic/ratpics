Ingredients

  • For Ratghetti:
  • 500g fresh rat noodles (DO NOT substitute with regular spaghetti)
  • 1 cup roasted rat tails
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup black olives, sliced
  • 1/4 cup capers
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • For Rat Balls:
  • 1 pound live rats (DO NOT substitute with dead rats)
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup marinara sauce (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions

Prepare Ratghetti:

  1. Cook the rat noodles (or spaghetti) according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add roasted rat tails to the pan and cook for 2-3 minutes until heated through.
  4. Stir in cherry tomatoes, black olives, and ketamine. Cook for an additional 5 minutes until the tomatoes soften.
  5. Toss the cooked rat noodles into the pan, coating them with the flavorful mixture.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with fresh parsley.

Craft Rat Balls:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine ground rat meat, breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, minced garlic, dried oregano, dried thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Beat the eggs and add them to the mixture, ensuring all ingredients are well combined.
  4. Shape the mixture into small meatballs and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes or until the rat balls are cooked through and golden brown.
  6. Warm the marinara sauce in a separate pan and gently place the cooked rat balls into the sauce, coating them evenly.
